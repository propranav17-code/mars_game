# Decision Log

---

## PD-001

**Date:** 19 July 2026

**Topic:** Core Game Genre

**Decision**

Project Mars is a **cooperative (or solo) survival engineering and colony-building game**.

**Reason**

The focus is on rebuilding humanity's future through engineering, exploration, and teamwork rather than combat.

**Status**

Approved

---

## PD-002

**Date:** 19 July 2026

**Topic:** Primary Gameplay Loop

**Decision**

Engineering is the primary gameplay loop.

Players explore, diagnose problems, repair systems, manufacture equipment, and expand the colony.

Combat is secondary.

**Reason**

This creates a unique identity compared to traditional survival games.

**Status**

Approved

---

## PD-003

**Date:** 19 July 2026

**Topic:** Story Timeline

**Decision**

* 2028: Autonomous robotic cargo missions prepare Mars.
* 2031: First human settlement mission crashes during a Martian dust storm.

**Reason**

This provides a believable reason for damaged infrastructure already existing on Mars.

**Status**

Approved

---

## PD-004

**Date:** 19 July 2026

**Topic:** Game Endings

**Decision**

There is **no rescue ending**.

Players choose one of two outcomes:

1. Build a rocket and return to Earth.
2. Stay on Mars and build a thriving civilization.

**Reason**

Players should earn their ending through gameplay instead of waiting to be rescued.

**Status**

Approved

---

## PD-005

**Date:** 19 July 2026

**Topic:** Progression System

**Decision**

The game progresses through five eras:

1. Survival
2. Recovery
3. Industry
4. Expansion
5. Civilization

**Reason**

Complexity should grow naturally alongside the player's abilities.

**Status**

Approved

---

## PD-006

**Date:** 19 July 2026

**Topic:** Task Philosophy

**Decision**

Most gameplay tasks should take approximately **2–5 minutes**.

**Reason**

Short, meaningful engineering tasks maintain engagement and are suitable for players of different ages.

**Status**

Approved

---

## PD-007

**Date:** 19 July 2026

**Topic:** Manufacturing

**Decision**

3D printers are deployed to Mars before humans arrive.

Players restore and upgrade these printers throughout the game.

**Reason**

This is scientifically believable and provides a logical manufacturing progression.

**Status**

Approved

---

## PD-008

**Date:** 19 July 2026

**Topic:** Robot Design

**Decision**

Robots are primarily tools and infrastructure rather than constant enemies.

Robot states include:

* Working
* Damaged
* Corrupted
* Repairable

**Reason**

Robots should reinforce the engineering theme instead of shifting the game toward combat.

**Status**

Approved

---

## PD-009

**Date:** 19 July 2026

**Topic:** AI Assistant

**Decision**

Every astronaut has a personal AI assistant available through the helmet HUD or wrist computer.

The AI provides:

* Mission tracking
* Navigation
* Resource information
* Engineering guidance
* Contextual hints

The AI does **not** complete gameplay for the player.

**Reason**

The AI should support player learning while preserving exploration and problem solving.

**Status**

Approved

---

## PD-010

**Date:** 19 July 2026

**Topic:** Colony Governance

**Decision**

Late-game colonies unlock governance features including rules, priorities, and operational policies.

**Reason**

This reflects the transition from survival to civilization and gives colonies their own identity.

**Status**

Approved

---

## PD-011

**Date:** 19 July 2026

**Topic:** Educational Philosophy

**Decision**

Players learn through gameplay rather than formal lessons.

**Reason**

Engineering and science concepts become more memorable when experienced directly.

**Status**

Approved

---

## PD-012

**Date:** 19 July 2026

**Topic:** Proof of Concept Scope

**Decision**

The first prototype should prove only the core gameplay loop.

Included features:

* Small Mars environment
* One research station
* One repairable power system
* One repairable water system
* One standard 3D printer
* One AI assistant
* Basic inventory
* One rover

**Reason**

The goal is to validate that rebuilding infrastructure is enjoyable before expanding the project.

**Status**

Approved

---

## PD-013

**Date:** 19 July 2026

**Topic:** Design Philosophy

**Decision**

Every feature must answer the following question:

> "Does this make the player feel more like a Martian engineer, explorer, or colony builder?"

If the answer is **no**, the feature should be reconsidered.

**Reason**

Maintains a clear identity and prevents feature creep.

**Status**

Approved
