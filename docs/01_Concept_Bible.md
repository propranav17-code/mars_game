# Project Vision

> **Project Mars is a cooperative (or solo) survival engineering game where players rebuild humanity's future on Mars through exploration, engineering, teamwork, and scientific discovery.**

The game is not about defeating enemies.

It is about solving problems.

Players begin as survivors of a catastrophic landing and gradually transform a damaged robotic research network into the first thriving human civilization on Mars—or build a rocket and return to Earth.

---

# Vision Statement

Every gameplay system should answer one question:

> **"Does this make the player feel like a Martian engineer, explorer, and colony builder?"**

If the answer is **yes**, the feature belongs.

If the answer is **no**, reconsider it.

---

# Story

## 2028

Several unmanned cargo missions successfully land on Mars.

Their purpose is to prepare the planet for humanity.

These missions deploy:

* Autonomous construction robots
* Solar farms
* Scientific laboratories
* Habitat modules
* Water extraction systems
* Ice pipelines
* Communication towers
* Manufacturing facilities
* Standard and industrial 3D printers
* Exploration rovers

For three years, the robots construct the first Martian research network.

---

## 2031

Humanity launches its first permanent settlement mission.

During landing, an unexpected global Martian dust storm destabilizes the descent.

The landing vehicle crashes.

The spacecraft is destroyed.

Only a handful of astronauts survive.

Although the robotic infrastructure still exists, much of it is damaged, buried, or offline.

The future of humanity now depends on the players.

---

# Player Objectives

Players create their own path.

There are two primary endings.

## Ending A – Return to Earth

Restore industrial capability.

Repair manufacturing.

Upgrade production facilities.

Construct a launch vehicle.

Manufacture fuel.

Launch successfully.

The player earns the journey home.

---

## Ending B – Build a Civilization

Instead of leaving Mars,

players choose to stay.

Expand habitats.

Restore agriculture.

Automate production.

Create laws and governance.

Transform the colony into humanity's first permanent settlement beyond Earth.

# The Five Eras

## Era 1 – Survival

Objectives:

* Stay alive
* Restore power
* Secure oxygen
* Find shelter
* Learn basic systems

Objectives:

* Repair damaged stations
* Restore pipelines
* Repair standard 3D printers
* Recover robots
* Reactivate infrastructure

Objectives:

* Produce advanced components

* Build new habitats
* Expand transportation
* Automate logistics
* Grow the colony beyond survival

# Engineering Philosophy

Engineering is the core gameplay.

Tasks should not feel like repetitive chores.

Instead, every task should solve a meaningful problem.

Examples include:

* Repairing an oxygen generator
* Replacing damaged solar controllers
* Restoring a water pipeline
* Printing replacement tools
* Repairing robots
* Upgrading manufacturing equipment

---

# Task Philosophy

Most gameplay tasks should take approximately **2–5 minutes**.

Players should understand:

* What is broken
* Why it matters
* How to repair it

Complexity comes from decision-making and teamwork, not difficult controls.

---

# Manufacturing

Manufacturing is one of Project Mars' defining mechanics.

Robots transported and assembled 3D printers before humans arrived.

Players restore these facilities over time.

### Standard Printers

Produce:

* Repair tools
* Connectors
* Pipe fittings
* Replacement components

### Industrial Printers

Produce:

* Robot parts
* Vehicle components
* Habitat structures
* Manufacturing equipment
* Rocket components

---

# AI Assistant

Every astronaut has an integrated AI assistant accessible through a helmet HUD or wrist computer.

The AI can:

* Display mission objectives
* Monitor health and resources
* Explain systems
* Suggest repair requirements
* Identify missing components
* Guide exploration

The AI provides assistance but never completes gameplay for the player.

---

# Colony Governance

As the colony grows, players unlock governance.

Examples include:

* Resource priorities
* Robot work priorities
* Colony rules
* Operational procedures

These systems allow every colony to develop its own identity.

---

# Educational Philosophy

Project Mars teaches through interaction.

Players naturally learn about:

* Engineering
* Robotics
* Manufacturing
* Energy systems
* Agriculture
* Space exploration

The game never pauses to lecture.

Learning happens while solving problems.

---

# Design Principles

* Engineering over combat.
* Every task has a purpose.
* Real science inspires gameplay.
* Teamwork creates memorable experiences.
* Players should feel continuous progress.
* Discovery should always be rewarding.
* Hope is stronger than fear.

---

# Proof of Concept Scope

The initial prototype should prove only the core gameplay loop.

The PoC should include:

* One small Mars map
* One damaged research station
* Basic movement
* Inventory
* One repairable power system
* One repairable water system
* One standard 3D printer
* One AI assistant
* One rover
* Basic crafting through the printer

The goal is **not** to build the full game.

The goal is to answer one question:

> **"Is rebuilding Mars through engineering fun?"**

If the answer is yes, the project moves to the next stage.

---

# Guiding Principle

Project Mars is not about surviving against monsters.

It is about rebuilding civilization.

Every repaired machine, every restored robot, every printed component, and every new habitat represents another step toward humanity's future on Mars.

Players should finish each session with one feeling:

> **"We built this."**
