# 1. Purpose

This document defines the official story and lore of Project Mars.

It establishes the world in which the player exists and explains why every gameplay mechanic exists.

The story should support gameplay rather than control it.

Project Mars is not a scripted story game.

It is a sandbox built on a believable history.

---

# 2. Narrative Philosophy

Project Mars follows four principles:

1. Real science inspires the world.
2. Engineering solves problems.
3. The player creates their own story.
4. Hope is stronger than fear.

The game should feel optimistic despite difficult situations.

---

# 3. Timeline

## 2026–2027

Several space agencies and private companies begin planning the first permanent human settlement on Mars.

International cooperation leads to the creation of the **Mars Settlement Initiative (MSI)**.

The objective is simple:

> Build humanity's first permanent off-world settlement.

---

## 2028 — Autonomous Deployment

Cargo missions successfully land on Mars.

No humans are present.

Instead, the missions deploy autonomous infrastructure including:

* Construction robots
* Maintenance robots
* Solar farms
* Scientific laboratories
* Habitation modules
* Water extraction systems
* Ice pipeline networks
* Communications towers
* Power distribution systems
* Manufacturing facilities
* Standard and industrial 3D printers
* Exploration rovers

Over the next three years, these robotic systems prepare Mars for human arrival.

---

## 2029–2030

Robotic construction continues.

Research Station Alpha becomes the first operational settlement.

Additional stations are constructed across nearby regions.

Most work is completed without direct human intervention.

---

## 2031 — Humanity Arrives

The first permanent human expedition launches from Earth.

Their objective:

Occupy the prepared settlement and begin permanent habitation.

During atmospheric descent, Mars experiences a severe global dust storm.

Although rare, such storms are scientifically possible.

Visibility drops dramatically.

Navigation becomes unreliable.

The landing vehicle crashes.

Several habitats are damaged.

Communications fail.

Supply containers scatter across the landscape.

The robotic infrastructure survives in varying condition.

Some systems continue operating.

Others shut down.

Many remain partially functional.

The future of the settlement now depends on the surviving astronauts.

---

# 4. The Beginning

The player regains consciousness shortly after the crash.

No dramatic cutscene is required.

No lengthy introduction is needed.

The first moments should communicate:

* Isolation
* Damage
* Opportunity

The player immediately begins exploring.

There are no objective markers.

There is no active AI.

The world itself teaches the player.

---

# 5. Research Station Alpha

Research Station Alpha serves as the player's initial home.

Originally built entirely by autonomous construction robots, it contains:

* Crew quarters
* Engineering workshop
* Medical bay
* Storage
* Power systems
* Water processing
* Communications center
* Operations center
* Standard manufacturing facilities

Most systems are damaged after the storm.

---

# 6. The Communications Blackout

One of the most significant consequences of the crash is the loss of communications.

Without power and communications:

* Earth cannot contact the player.
* The station AI remains offline.
* Engineering databases are inaccessible.
* Automated logistics stop functioning.

Restoring the Communications & Operations Center marks the end of the Emergency Era.

---

# 7. The AI

Every astronaut's suit is equipped with hardware capable of connecting to the station AI.

However, the AI software resides within the station's Operations Center.

Because that facility is offline, the player begins alone.

Once communications and operations are restored, the AI becomes available.

The AI serves as an engineering assistant rather than a mission commander.

---

# 8. Robots

Robots are essential members of the settlement.

They are designed for:

* Construction
* Maintenance
* Transportation
* Manufacturing
* Scientific research

Following the crash, robots exist in four states:

### Operational

Continue performing assigned duties.

### Dormant

Await power or instructions.

### Damaged

Require repairs before returning to service.

### Corrupted

Battery leaks, damaged electronics, or corrupted control software may cause unpredictable behavior.

Corrupted robots become environmental hazards.

They are not evil.

They are malfunctioning machines.

Whenever possible, repairing them is preferable to destroying them.

---

# 9. Humanity's Goal

The objective is not merely survival.

The objective is to determine humanity's future on Mars.

Players ultimately choose between:

### Return Home

Construct a launch vehicle.

Manufacture fuel.

Launch successfully.

Return to Earth.

---

### Stay on Mars

Expand the settlement.

Establish industry.

Create governance.

Build humanity's first permanent civilization beyond Earth.

Neither ending is considered the "correct" choice.

---

# 10. Exploration Philosophy

The player is never forced along a predefined path.

Instead, exploration reveals opportunities.

Example:

The player notices a leaking water pipe.

Repairing the pipe restores water.

Water enables agriculture.

Agriculture supports expansion.

Every repair naturally unlocks new possibilities.

---

# 11. Environmental Storytelling

The world should tell its own story.

Examples include:

* Half-finished construction projects.
* Maintenance robots frozen mid-task.
* Scattered supply containers.
* Personal belongings left behind.
* Damaged research equipment.
* Dust-covered solar farms.
* Maintenance logs.
* Engineering reports.

Players should learn about Mars by exploring rather than through lengthy dialogue.

---

# 12. Design Rules

Story should never interrupt gameplay.

Gameplay creates the story.

Players should remember:

"I repaired that station."

"I restored power."

"I built that colony."

These moments are the true narrative of Project Mars.

---

# 13. Future Expansion

Future updates may introduce:

* Additional research stations.
* New engineering challenges.
* Larger industrial facilities.
* Expanded colony history.
* Historical mission archives.
* New regions of Mars.

However:

No future content should contradict the principles established in this document.
