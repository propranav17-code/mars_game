# Purpose

This document defines the core gameplay loop for the Proof of Concept (PoC).

The objective is to validate that repairing and rebuilding a Mars research station is enjoyable before expanding into the full game.

---

# Core Gameplay Loop

The player repeatedly follows this cycle:

```
Explore
    ↓
Discover a Problem
    ↓
Understand the Problem
    ↓
Find Required Parts
    ↓
Repair the System
    ↓
Restore Functionality
    ↓
Unlock New Possibilities
    ↓
Repeat
```

Each completed repair should noticeably improve the station and unlock the next challenge.

---

# First Five Minutes

The player regains consciousness after the crash.

Objectives:

* Learn basic movement
* Explore the nearby area
* Reach Research Station Alpha
* Activate emergency power

The player should immediately understand that the station is damaged but recoverable.

---

# First Thirty Minutes

The player learns the core loop through three connected tasks:

1. Restore electrical power.
2. Restore water supply.
3. Repair the standard 3D printer.

Each repair unlocks the next task.

---

# First Hour

With power and water restored, the player:

* Prints a basic engineering tool.
* Repairs a rover.
* Explores a nearby location.
* Returns with additional resources.

By the end of the first hour, the player should feel ownership of the station.

---

# Player Progression

The player becomes more capable over time by restoring infrastructure rather than by leveling up.

Progress comes from:

* Better tools
* Better equipment
* Better stations
* Better robots
* Better manufacturing

---

# Reward Loop

Every completed task provides a visible reward.

Examples:

* Lights turn on.
* Doors unlock.
* Water flows.
* Robots reactivate.
* New crafting recipes become available.
* New areas become accessible.

The player should always see the direct impact of their work.

---

# Failure Loop

Failure should create new problems rather than immediate game over.

Examples:

* Running out of power disables equipment.
* Ignoring maintenance causes systems to fail.
* Dust storms damage exposed infrastructure.

Players are encouraged to recover, adapt, and continue.

---

# PoC Success Criteria

The Proof of Concept is successful if players:

* Understand the repair loop quickly.
* Enjoy solving engineering problems.
* Feel rewarded by restoring the station.
* Want to continue improving the colony.

If these goals are achieved, the game is ready to expand into larger systems.

---

# Guiding Principle

Every repair should make Mars feel a little more alive.

The player's actions should leave permanent, visible improvements to the world.
