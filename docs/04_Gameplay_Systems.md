# 1. Purpose

This document defines the immutable rules of the Project Mars universe.

These rules ensure that all gameplay systems, missions, code and future updates remain consistent.

If a new feature conflicts with this document, the feature must be redesigned before implementation.

---

# 2. Design Philosophy

Project Mars follows one guiding principle:

> **Engineering, exploration and problem solving are always more important than combat.**

Players progress by restoring systems rather than defeating enemies.

---

# 3. Scientific Realism

Project Mars is inspired by real science.

Where realism hurts gameplay, carefully chosen simplifications are allowed.

The goal is **believable**, not perfect simulation.

---

# 4. The World Never Cheats

The game should never create artificial difficulty.

Players fail because of decisions, preparation or engineering—not because the game unfairly changes rules.

---

# 5. No Aliens

Mars contains:

* No alien civilizations
* No monsters
* No supernatural events
* No magic

Every challenge comes from nature, engineering failures or human decisions.

---

# 6. Mars is Dangerous

Natural hazards include:

* Dust storms
* Radiation
* Low temperatures
* Equipment failure
* Limited resources
* Long travel distances

These hazards create gameplay without relying on enemies.

---

# 7. Resources Have Value

Every resource exists for a reason.

Examples:

Water

* Drinking
* Agriculture
* Manufacturing
* Fuel production

Power

* Lights
* Doors
* Printers
* Communications

Metal

* Repairs
* Structures
* Manufacturing

Nothing exists purely as "loot."

---

# 8. Information is Earned

Players begin with limited knowledge.

Knowledge is gained through:

* Exploration
* Repairing systems
* Recovering logs
* Activating databases
* Speaking with the AI

The game never reveals everything immediately.

---

# 9. Exploration First

Players are never forced along a linear path.

Instead:

Explore

↓

Observe

↓

Investigate

↓

Repair

↓

Unlock New Possibilities

Every repaired system naturally creates new opportunities.

---

# 10. Operations Replace Traditional Quests

Project Mars does not use typical quest systems.

Instead it uses:

Story Goal

↓

Operation

↓

Task

Example:

Story Goal

Build a Permanent Colony

↓

Operation

Restore Water

↓

Tasks

* Find Wrench
* Repair Pipe
* Replace Filter
* Restart Pump

Players may complete tasks in different orders whenever possible.

---

# 11. The AI Assistant

The AI is never available at the beginning.

It becomes operational only after:

* Communications restored
* Operations Center repaired
* Power stabilized

The AI is an engineering assistant.

It never solves problems automatically.

Its role is to:

* Explain
* Track
* Suggest
* Record

The player performs all critical work.

---

# 12. Robots

Robots are machines.

They have no emotions.

They perform assigned work.

Robot states:

Operational

Dormant

Damaged

Corrupted

Corrupted robots behave unpredictably because of hardware failures or software corruption.

Repair is preferred over destruction whenever possible.

---

# 13. Manufacturing

3D printers are central to colony growth.

Early printers produce:

* Hand tools
* Replacement parts
* Pipe fittings
* Basic equipment

Later upgrades unlock:

* Electronics
* Robot components
* Habitat modules
* Industrial machinery

Manufacturing becomes one of the primary progression systems.

---

# 14. Earth Communication

Communication with Earth follows realistic delay.

Messages are never instantaneous.

Earth cannot directly control the player.

The colony must solve immediate problems independently.

---

# 15. Death

Players can fail.

Failure has consequences.

Prototype:

* Reload last save

Future:

* Loss of resources
* Mission progress reset
* Equipment damage

Death should encourage better planning rather than punish experimentation.

---

# 16. Multiplayer

Designed for:

1–4 players

Players cooperate.

No PvP.

Players share:

* Resources
* Operations
* Manufacturing
* Colony progress

Every player contributes to rebuilding Mars.

---

# 17. Player Freedom

There is no single correct order.

Example:

Player A repairs water first.

Player B restores power first.

Player C repairs printers first.

Different paths should remain viable whenever possible.

---

# 18. Colony Progression

The colony develops through five eras.

## Era 0 — Emergency

No AI

No guidance

Learn by exploring

Repair basic systems

Restore Communications & Operations Center

---

## Era 1 — Recovery

AI Assistant Online

Engineering database restored

Operations become visible

Basic manufacturing begins

---

## Era 2 — Industry

Upgrade printers

Repair factories

Recover robots

Expand production

---

## Era 3 — Expansion

Build new habitats

Deploy autonomous robots

Explore farther from the original station

Create transportation networks

---

## Era 4 — Civilization

Create laws

Govern the colony

Choose Mars' future

Remain on Mars or prepare a return to Earth

---

# 19. World Progression

The world changes because of the player.

Examples:

Power restored

↓

Lights turn on

Doors unlock

Machines activate

Robots return

Plants grow

The player's actions permanently improve the colony.

---

# 20. Asset Philosophy

Graphics support gameplay.

Gameplay never depends on expensive assets.

Temporary placeholder models are acceptable during development.

Mechanics are always implemented before visual polish.

---

# 21. Developer Rules

When implementing features:

1. Simplicity first.
2. Gameplay before graphics.
3. Exploration before tutorials.
4. Engineering before combat.
5. Reward curiosity.
6. Prefer systems over scripted events.

---

# 22. Future Expansion

The following may be added later without violating this document:

* Additional stations
* New industries
* More robot types
* Weather improvements
* Better manufacturing
* Larger colonies
* Advanced automation

The following are permanently excluded:

* Aliens
* Magic
* Superpowers
* Fantasy technology

---

# Developer Notes

This document is the constitutional rulebook for Project Mars.

Every future document, gameplay system and code implementation should comply with these rules.

Any exception must be documented and approved before implementation.
